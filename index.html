Pannya Metta Sangha

Pannya Metta Sangha (PMS) is a spiritual and social organization dedicated to promoting wisdom, loving-kindness, and community service. Through our various initiatives, we strive to create a harmonious society built on the principles of compassion, understanding, and collective growth.

Our mission is to spread the teachings of mindfulness and ethical living while actively engaging in social welfare activities that benefit communities. We welcome all who seek to walk the path of wisdom and compassion.

Our organization welcomes people from all backgrounds to participate in our activities, learn from our teachings, and contribute to building a more compassionate world.

Mahasamadhi Bhoomi

Mahasamadhi Bhumi is a prominent Buddhist spiritual and cultural site situated at Ruyad (Sindhpuri) village near Pauni, in Bhandara district of Maharashtra, India, on the serene banks of the Wainganga River. The site was established on 8 February 1987 with the objective of preserving, promoting, and revitalizing Buddha Dhamma and the values of peace, compassion, and wisdom. Pauni is an ancient town and has been recognized as one of the major centers of Buddhist culture since the time of Emperor Ashoka, a legacy that is still evident today through historical remains and continuing traditions.

Spread across approximately 6.5 acres of land, the complex is home to the magnificent Maha Samadhi Bhumi Maha Stupa, a huge and visually striking structure erected on the riverbank. The stupa, inaugurated on 8 February 2007, is regarded as one of the finest contemporary examples of Buddhist architecture and sculpture in the region. Its design and artistic elements depict significant events from the life of the Buddha, offering a spiritual narrative through art and creating an atmosphere of deep reverence and reflection.

Since its establishment, Mahasamadhi Bhumi has evolved into an important Buddhist pilgrimage destination in central India. Thousands of monks, devotees, and visitors from India and abroad visit the site every year, especially during Dhamma Mahotsav and on Poornima (full moon) and Amavasya (new moon) days. These gatherings involve meditation, prayer, and Dhamma-related activities, reinforcing the site‚Äôs role as a living center of Buddhist practice and cultural heritage.

Pannya Metta Balsadan

Pannya Metta Balsadan is dedicated to provide a stable, loving family atmosphere. To support orphan children who are unable to live on their own until they become self-sufficient, to provide them with all education and prepare them for life with compassion of family. It is located at Sindhpuri, Pauni, Dist. Bhandara (95 kms. from Nagpur), Maharashtra, India, in associations self-owned premises. Balsadan is established on a land of 9 acres very close to Mahasamadhi Bhoomi, in the year 1995, on February, 8th. Which is well equipped with all facilities. Association provides food, healthcare, educational material & fulfill their all necessity requirements of orphans on priority. The work started in this Balsadan comprising of 16 Halls. Initially, there were only 7 childrens and now it is 32, of which 14 are boys and 18 girls. Only the orphans, facing poor conditions, having no parents and neglected by the society are given shelter in balsadan, irrespective of castes, creeds and religions. Not only these children are educated, but also they are growth-wise and culture-wise sculptured here. Two Superintendents, two teachers for educating, a music teacher, assistant, cook, watchman etc. are among the others seen there. The social, cultural and thought provoking programs are regularly conducted over there. Children performing on stage on 8th Feb 2017 on occasion of Pannya Metta Balsadan‚Äôs Anniversary. The children over there feel just at home. Benefit of Organization every year cloths & bedding material are donated to this Balsadan by Pannya Metta Sangha.

All the children are doing their education in a reputed Primary, Middle/ High school & Colleges and have the opportunity to take part in activities, such as dancing, singing and sports etc. Those children are weak in their studies, special attention are paid towards them by personal instructor at Balsadan, while others all are studying well, getting good marks and ranks in their classes.
